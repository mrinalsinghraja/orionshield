Common questions

Does OrionShield send any of my data to a server?+
No. All scans, AI summaries, and clipboard analysis run entirely on your device. Nothing is uploaded, logged, or transmitted to any external server — ever.
Why is Clipboard Guardian off by default?+
Clipboard monitoring is disabled by default to protect your privacy. You choose to enable it in Settings. Once on, analysis stays on your device only and clipboard contents are never stored to disk.
What does "heuristic threat review" mean?+
OrionShield scans the areas it can see within the system sandbox and surfaces signals that look unusual. A clean result means no high-confidence indicators were found in visible areas — not a guarantee of complete system safety. We're always upfront about this.
Why does it show fewer permissions than I expected?+
OrionShield runs inside the system sandbox, which limits what it can inspect. It only shows permission signals it can directly verify for itself. It never fabricates records for other apps — that's by design.
Is OrionShield a full antivirus?+
No. OrionShield is a security review and guidance tool. It surfaces visible signals and explains them in plain language. It does not claim complete system-wide malware coverage and we think that honesty is a feature, not a limitation.
How do I remove a suspicious startup item?+
OrionShield shows you which startup items look unusual and explains why. To remove one, open System Settings → General → Login Items and remove it there. OrionShield never removes anything without your explicit action.
The AI Assistant isn't responding — what should I do?+
The AI Assistant uses the on-device language model. If it is unavailable on your device or system version, OrionShield automatically falls back to deterministic rule-based summaries. No data is sent to external servers either way.
